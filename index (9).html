Afro-Carib Hub was born from a simple belief — that Black and Caribbean excellence in Philadelphia deserves to be seen, celebrated, and supported.

🌍
Our Mission

Connecting Culture to Commerce

We created Afro-Carib Hub to bridge the gap between the vibrant Black and Caribbean community in Philadelphia and the incredible businesses that serve it.

Every dollar spent at a local Black or Caribbean-owned business is an investment in our community. We make it easier for people to find, support, and celebrate those businesses every single day.

From the jerk spots in West Philly to the natural hair salons in Germantown, from Caribbean festivals in North Philly to Black-owned law firms downtown — we're putting it all on the map.

The Story Behind the Hub

Why We Built This

It started with a frustrating experience. Trying to find a Caribbean restaurant in Philly shouldn't require scrolling through pages of generic review sites only to find outdated info, closed businesses, and listings that have nothing to do with the culture you're looking for.

We kept asking the same question in our community: "Where do I find a good Jamaican spot near me?" or "Do you know any Black-owned accountants in West Philly?" The answer was always someone's personal contact list or a Facebook group post that got buried in hours.

"Our community has always been the engine behind Philadelphia's culture. It was time we built something that reflects that."

— The Afro-Carib Hub Founding Team

So we built Afro-Carib Hub. A dedicated, community-powered space where Black and Caribbean businesses in Philadelphia get the visibility they deserve, and where our community can quickly find exactly what they need — from jerk chicken to justice, from locs to legal advice.

We're just getting started. And we're building this with the community, not just for it.
